Frequently asked questions

Everything you might want to know before you switch to Standly.

I use forScore on iPad. Is there a Windows equivalent?

Yes, that's exactly why we built Standly. If you've moved to a Surface or another Windows tablet, you don't have to give up a polished music-stand experience. Standly is a native Windows app with PDF viewing, pen and touch annotations, setlists, and hands-free Bluetooth page turns, built specifically for Windows rather than ported from another platform.

Does Standly work on a Surface Pro or other Windows tablet?

Yes. Standly runs on any Windows 10 or 11 device, including Surface Pro, Surface Go, and other tablets and 2-in-1s, as well as laptops and desktops. It works on Intel and AMD machines and on ARM-based Windows devices such as the Surface Pro X and Copilot+ PCs. It's designed for touch and pen first, with smooth high-DPI rendering, so it feels right with a tablet on your music stand.

How is Standly different from other sheet music apps?

Most music apps on Windows are ports or web wrappers with custom interfaces that don't behave like a normal Windows app. Standly is built natively for Windows, so it uses familiar controls, real keyboard shortcuts, and proper pen and touch support, with nothing new to learn. It's also fully offline and a one-time purchase, with no accounts and no subscription.

Is Standly a subscription or a one-time purchase?

Standly is a one-time purchase, with no subscription. Buy it once and the full music stand is yours. Down the road we may add optional advanced features (such as automatic music recognition) as a separate offering, but the music stand itself stays a one-time purchase.

Does Standly need an account or an internet connection?

No. Standly works completely offline, with no account or sign-in required. Your sheet music and annotations stay on your device, and there's no telemetry tracking what you play. Once it's installed, you never need a connection to use it.

What file formats can Standly open?

Standly opens standard PDF sheet music today, the format most of your library is probably already in. Support for image files (scans and photos of music) is coming soon.

Can I mark up my music with a pen or touch, and do my annotations save?

Yes. Annotate directly on the page with ink, highlighters, and an eraser, using a pen, your finger, or a mouse. Your markings save automatically and stay with the music across sessions, so they're there every time you reopen the piece.

If I move or rename my files, do my annotations stay with them?

Yes. Standly tracks each piece by its content, not just its file path, so your ink, highlights, and notes stay attached even if you move a file to a different folder or rename it. We put a lot of work into this, so you can reorganize your library however you like without any fear of losing your markup. Your annotations follow the music.

Can I bring my annotations and markup from another app into Standly?

Yes. If you've already marked up your sheet music in another app, Standly can import your annotations, notes, and other markup, so switching doesn't mean starting over. We're actively expanding the list of apps and formats we support, and we'd love to hear what you use, so send us your requests.

Which Bluetooth page turners or foot pedals does Standly support?

Standly supports popular Bluetooth controllers including AirTurn and Flic, and is likely compatible with other Bluetooth page-turn devices as well. You can customize which button turns the page in each direction to match your device.

Can I organize pieces into setlists for a performance?

Yes. Build setlists to put pieces in performance order, restrict them to specific page ranges, and move seamlessly from one document to the next, so your whole program flows without hunting for the next chart.

What do I need to run Standly?

Standly runs on Windows 10 (version 1809 or later) and Windows 11, on any PC, laptop, tablet, or 2-in-1, with both Intel or AMD and ARM-based processors supported. It works with mouse, touch, and pen, so a touchscreen is great for annotating but isn't required.

Can I try Standly before buying?

Yes, Standly comes with a free 30-day trial, so you can use it on your own device with your real music and your own pedal before deciding. After the trial, it's a single one-time purchase.
